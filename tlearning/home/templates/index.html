{% block title %} Introduction to the world of my thesis {% endblock %}

My P.h.D thesis is about Uncertain labels in classification and expert feedback. And in this subject, I particularly focus on playing with the logistic regression.

"Why?" you ask, well take a look at what modern deep learning models look like (just on your right), that will give you a hint.

Do you have it now? Yes, classification neural networks in general are simply a big black box at which we add a linear layer. And because most of the time we use the cross-entropy loss (see this part) this results in doing a complicated thing and then a simple logistic regression.

Here you will find:
  • Some reminders about the linear model, because we all need to start from the beginning to go further,
  • A crash course on logistic regression, and some visualisations, the thesis being essentially on the role of the logistic regression in models with uncertainty, that should be a good place to freshen up with this model,
  • Some applications to visualize what we mean by uncertainty followed by some explainations about the "in practice". Don't hesitate to play with them (and report here if you break it)
